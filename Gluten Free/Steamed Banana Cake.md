# Steamed Banana Cake (Bánh Chuối Hấp)
(Reference Video) [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5rewbEst4C4] 

## Introduction

Hello everyone!  
Today I’m sharing how to make this **extremely delicious steamed banana cake**.

I learned this recipe from a woman who sells sweet soups (*chè*) at the local market. I’ve been her regular customer for many years, and she generously shared her recipe with me. Her **secret lies in the way the bananas are cooked (caramelized) and steamed**, which is quite different from usual methods.

The result is a banana cake that is **soft, chewy, slightly elastic, yet has a pleasant crunchy bite**. It’s incredibly tasty—honestly, there’s nothing to criticize about it.

This cake is great not only for eating at home but also for selling, since many people really love it. So let’s head into the kitchen and make it together!

---

## Ingredients

### For the Banana Cake
- 800 g ripe bananas (I use *chuối sim*, but any ripe banana works)
- 80 g yellow sugar (for better color)
- A little water (to help dissolve the sugar)
- 300 g tapioca starch
- 30 g rice flour (about 3 tablespoons)
- 250 ml warm water (for mixing batter)
- 250 ml water (for cooked batter starter)
- Oil (for greasing molds)

### For Serving (Optional)
- Coconut milk
- Roasted peanuts
- Toasted sesame seeds

---

## Step 1: Prepare and Cook the Bananas

1. Peel the bananas. Use **fully ripe bananas** so the cake is sweet and not astringent.
2. Place bananas into a pan.
3. Add:
   - 80 g yellow sugar  
   - A little water
4. Cook over **medium heat**, stirring gently.

**Goal:**
- Bananas soften
- Sugar thickens and caramelizes, creating color and aroma

When the sugar becomes sticky and glossy and the bananas are soft, turn off the heat and let them cool slightly.

---

## Step 2: Cut the Bananas

- **Half the bananas** → finely chop or shred  
- **Other half** → slice thinly (for topping)

> Tip: Thinner slices make the cake softer and tastier.

---

## Step 3: Mix the Batter

In a bowl, combine:
- 300 g tapioca starch  
- 30 g rice flour  
- 250 ml warm water  

Mix until smooth.

---

## Step 4: Make the Cooked Batter Starter (Key Technique)

This step keeps the cake soft and chewy all day.

1. Scoop **1 tablespoon** of batter into a saucepan.
2. Add **250 ml water**.
3. Cook over **low–medium heat**, stirring constantly.
4. When the mixture turns translucent and boils evenly, turn off the heat.
5. Pour this hot mixture back into the raw batter and mix well.

**Result:**
- Batter is slightly thick but still pourable
- Cake is soft, chewy, glossy, and easy to unmold

---

## Step 5: Add the Bananas

1. Add the **finely chopped bananas** into the batter and mix well.
2. For banana slices (topping):
   - Add a ladle of batter
   - Toss to lightly coat  
   This prevents the slices from popping out later.

---

## Step 6: Prepare the Mold

1. Bring steamer water to a boil.
2. Place the mold in the steamer for **1 minute** to heat.
3. Lightly grease with oil.

---

## Step 7: Steam the Cake in Layers (Very Important)

⚠️ Do **not** pour all batter at once.

Steam in **3 thin layers**, similar to *bánh da lợn*.

### Layer 1
- Pour a thin layer of batter
- Steam **~3 minutes**, until slightly translucent

### Layer 2
- Add another thin layer
- Steam **~3 minutes**

### Layer 3 (Final Layer)
- Pour last layer
- Reduce heat slightly
- Arrange banana slices evenly on top

Dip slices in batter if needed so they are fully coated.

---

## Step 8: Final Steaming

- Return heat to **medium**
- Steam for **15 minutes total**
- Release steam once during cooking

### Check for Doneness
- Insert a skewer:
  - Comes out clear → done
- Banana slices should remain embedded and flat

---

## Step 9: Unmold and Slice

1. Brush a thin layer of oil on top.
2. Run a spatula around the edges.
3. Remove gently.

**You’ll notice:**
- Cake is soft and elastic
- Releases easily
- Layers fuse together without separating

Slice to reveal a glossy, chewy interior.

---

## Storage Notes

- Stays **soft even the next day**
- Caramelized bananas help it last longer
- More fragrant than using raw bananas

---

## Serving Suggestions

Delicious on its own, even better with:
- Coconut milk
- Roasted peanuts
- Toasted sesame seeds

---

## Coconut Milk Sauce (Nước Cốt Dừa)

### Ingredients
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 tablespoon tapioca starch
- ½ tablespoon rice flour
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- 400 ml coconut milk (thick + thin combined)

### Instructions

1. Mix sugar, tapioca starch, rice flour, and salt.
2. Add coconut milk and stir well.
3. Cook over **low heat**, stirring constantly.
4. Once boiling evenly, cook **2–3 more minutes**.
5. When translucent, turn off heat.

**Result:**
- Creamy, stable sauce
- Does not separate
- Stores well
